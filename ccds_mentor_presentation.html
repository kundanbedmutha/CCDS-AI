The Problem We're Solving

🎯
Prediction ≠ Action
Modern AI tells us WHAT will happen — a patient will deteriorate, a loan will default. It does NOT tell us WHY or most importantly WHAT TO DO about it.
🔗
XAI Without Causality
SHAP and LIME produce correlation-based feature importance. Telling a clinician "BMI is important" gives them nothing actionable. They need causal drivers, not correlates.
🚫
Infeasible Counterfactuals
DiCE and similar methods generate "what-if" scenarios that ignore causal structure. "Increase credit score by 50 points" is useless if spending behaviour causes credit score.
📊
No Intervention Priority
Existing frameworks dump a list of counterfactuals but never say which to act on first, with what expected effect, or at what cost. Decision-makers are left guessing.

04 —

Research Gaps — Before & After

G1

Counterfactuals Without Causality

DiCE/Wachter methods find minimal changes without respecting causal relationships. Recommending "reduce debt AND increase income simultaneously" ignores that they are causally linked.

→ SCM + PC Algorithm integrated into CFE pipeline. All recommendations constrained by learned DAG.
G2

No Intervention Prioritization

Existing methods output a flat set of counterfactuals. Decision-makers manually interpret multiple outputs with no ranking by feasibility or expected impact.

→ IPE (Intervention Prioritization Engine) ranks by actionability, proximity, causal effect size, and robustness.
G3

No Causal Fidelity Metric

All existing metrics (proximity, sparsity, validity) are computational but not causally grounded. Keane et al. (2021) confirmed this gap explicitly.

→ CCF (Causal Counterfactual Fidelity) — our novel metric measuring alignment with do-calculus-derived effects.
G4

Poor Baseline Coverage

v3 only compared against 3 baselines, missing CARLA which is a standard benchmark in the algorithmic recourse literature.

→ v4 adds CARLA-Style as 5th baseline across all 3 datasets. All comparisons remain statistically significant (p<0.001) on German Credit and Adult Income.
G5

Only 2 Datasets (v3 Limitation)

v3 had null results on Pima with no explanation, and only 2 domains — insufficient for generalisation claims in a publication.

→ v4 adds Adult Income (AUC=0.874). Pima null results explained as domain compression — a scientific finding now visualised in Fig 8.
G6

Robustness Not Evaluated

Proposal mentioned robustness in the IPE formula (w₄·Robustness) but v3 had no actual robustness evaluation across model variants.

→ v4 evaluates recourse robustness across 3 model variants (GBT + RF + LR). CCDS achieves 0.842 robustness on German Credit vs 0.507 for DiCE.
